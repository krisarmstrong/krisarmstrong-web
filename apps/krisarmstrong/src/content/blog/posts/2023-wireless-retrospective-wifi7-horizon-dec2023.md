## Challenges That Persisted

### Building Penetration

6 GHz propagation characteristics require design accommodation:

- 3-6 dB additional path loss vs 5 GHz
- Concrete/metal especially problematic
- Requires more APs or strategic placement
- Not solvable, only accommodated

### Battery Impact

6 GHz radios consume more power:

- 10-15% higher consumption typical
- Impacts mobile device battery life
- TWT helps but doesn't eliminate
- Trade-off for performance

### Legacy Device Support

Not all devices support 6 GHz:

- Requires dual-SSID strategies
- Complicates management
- Transition timeline varies by org
- Will persist for years

## Closing Thoughts: A Transformative Year

2023 will be remembered as the year Wi-Fi 6E matured from emerging technology to enterprise standard. The clean 6 GHz spectrum, combined with mandatory WPA3 and lessons learned from three years of deployments, has created an unprecedented foundation for wireless networking.

The numbers speak clearly: 6 GHz delivers measurable improvements in throughput, latency, reliability, and efficiency. The ecosystem has reached critical mass with broad client support, competitive pricing, and proven ROI. Organizations deploying Wi-Fi 6E in 2023 report consistent success across diverse use cases and industries.

As we close 2023 and look toward 2024, the horizon holds exciting promise. Wi-Fi 7 will bring revolutionary capabilities—particularly Multi-Link Operation—that will push wireless networking into new territory. But Wi-Fi 6E's maturity means organizations need not wait. The technology works exceptionally well today.

For network professionals, 2023 provided invaluable lessons: the importance of systematic design, the value of automation, the reality of AI capabilities, and the power of converged architectures. These lessons will serve us well as Wi-Fi 7 arrives and the industry continues its relentless innovation.

The question for 2024 isn't whether wireless technology can meet enterprise demands—2023 proved it can. The question is how quickly organizations can adopt mature technologies like Wi-Fi 6E while preparing infrastructure for the Wi-Fi 7 revolution just ahead.

Here's to a transformative 2023 and an even more exciting 2024 in enterprise wireless networking.
